Dexterity ellipsoid.

The Jacobian matrix is in the form of:
\begin{eqnarray}
\begin{Bmatrix}
\dot{P_x} \\
\dot{P_y} \\
\dot{P_z} \\
\omega_x \\
\omega_y \\
\omega_z
\end{Bmatrix} &=&
\begin{bmatrix}
J \\
6 \times 2
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{Bmatrix}
\dot{\theta}_1 \\
\dot{\theta}_2
\end{Bmatrix}
\end{eqnarray}

The Jacobian matrix for this problem is:
\begin{eqnarray}
J&=&\begin{Bmatrix}
\vec{a}_0 \times \parens{\vec{P}_E-\vec{P}_{0}} 	&
\vec{a}_1 \times \parens{\vec{P}_E-\vec{P}_{1}} 	\\
\vec{a}_0& \vec{a}_1 \\
\end{Bmatrix}
\end{eqnarray}
